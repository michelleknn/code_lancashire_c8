About me

I'm currently a Psychology MSc student at Lancaster University. My research focuses on the role of working memory in how we learn and direct attention (i.e., eye gaze pattern) under uncertainty.

Prior to my MSc, I studied Social Science (BSc) for two years before transferring to a Psychology BSc at Lancaster University. During which, I completed three reserach placements assisting research activites. From running online studies to in person experiments with both infants and adults. Most of which employed eye-tracking measures.
